Jade Michael Thornton

Hi there!

I'm a software engineer, hobby aerial photographer and weather enthusiast in the Twin Cities, Minnesota. I know some things about some software, and a lot about taking care of house rabbits. My favorite language is Clojure, but I never get to work in it. I'm currently a senior software engineer and tech lead at DroneDeploy, leading our growth engineering team.

I've previously worked at FlightAware (acquired by Collins Aerospace), Thomson Reuters, Maverick Software, and the Humphrey School of Public Affairs. I've previously studied computer science at the University of Minnesota and Saint Paul College, and I'm currently furthering my computer science and philosophy education through part-time study at the University of Minnesota.

Some other links include: my GitHub and GitLab profiles, my miniscule YouTube channel, my blog posts, some online tools, my LinkedIn profile, and some photos I took.

Memento mori.

My Projects

What I cannot create, I do not understand

All of my projects are open source and live on GitLab and/or GitHub. Here's some of my favorite highlights.

  • Emacs tools and configuration

    I use Emacs every day and love it, and I've put care and effort into crafting my development environment to a high level of productivity. I use a literate Emacs configuration, and I've authored several packages such as el2md, Rivet mode and several more.

  • Themes and more themes

    I have a desire for everything I use to look good and work well. To that end, I often create or contribute to themes of various types, including Aero (Emacs), Vero (Zsh), ClearNight (Atom, RIP) and Unbroken (GTK3). I also helped in the creation of the mini.css framework.

  • Languages

    I've also explored writing my own languages, though I've never completed one. Some of my half languages include Warbler, Brewlisp and Spider.

  • Education

    I enjoy teaching and mentoring new developers, and I have contributed to both FreeCodeCamp lessons and Learn X in Y minutes.

  • Miscellaneous contributions

    I was one of the authors of Armadietto (the official Node.js reference RemoteStorage server). For a time I was the lead maintainer of http-server, and a contributor to its major dependencies. I have also been a contributor to Alacritty (a terminal emulator), Brave Browser (prior to the 1.0 release), Tohil (a TCL-Python integration library), Formative (a Clojure web form renderer), and Zplug (a Zsh plugin manager).
